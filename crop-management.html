Crop Management

Sustainable practices for Ghana's major staple crops

üåΩ Maize Cultivation

Best Planting Time

Major season: March-April; Minor season: September-October

Recommended Varieties

Obatanpa, Abontem, Mamaba, Omankwa (drought-tolerant, high-yielding)

Fertilizer Application

Base: NPK 15-15-15 at 125kg/hectare at planting
Top dress: Urea at 150kg/hectare after 4 weeks

Pest Management

Monitor for fall armyworm. Use pheromone traps or neem-based pesticides.

üåø Cassava Production

Improved Varieties

Bankyehemaa, Afisiafi, Abrabopa, Essam Bankye (high-yielding, disease-resistant)

Planting Method

Use healthy mature stems (25cm length). Plant at 1m x 1m spacing. Plant at onset of major rains.

Weed Control

Weed at 3 and 8 weeks after planting. Mulching recommended for moisture retention.

Harvesting

12-18 months after planting depending on variety.

üçÖ Vegetable Farming

Tomato Production

Nursery: Raise seedlings for 4 weeks. Transplant at 60cm x 40cm spacing. Varieties: Power, Rasta

Pepper Production

Varieties: Legon 18, Kpakpo shito. Nursery period: 5-6 weeks. Space at 50cm x 40cm.

Irrigation

Drip irrigation recommended. Water requirement: 25-30mm per week. Mulching reduces water loss.

Integrated Pest Management

Use yellow sticky traps for whiteflies. Practice crop rotation.

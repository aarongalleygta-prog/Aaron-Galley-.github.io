Crop Management

Sustainable practices for Ghana's major staple crops

üåΩ Maize Cultivation

Best Planting Time

Major season: March-April; Minor season: September-October

Recommended Varieties

Obatanpa, Abontem, Mamaba, Omankwa (all drought-tolerant and high-yielding)

Fertilizer Application

Base: NPK 15-15-15 at 125kg/hectare at planting
Top dress: Sulfate of Ammonia or Urea at 150kg/hectare after 4 weeks

Pest Management

Monitor for fall armyworm. Use pheromone traps, neem-based pesticides, or recommended chemicals from MoFA extension agents.

üåø Cassava Production

Improved Varieties

Bankyehemaa, Afisiafi, Abrabopa, Essam Bankye (high-yielding, disease-resistant, CSSVD-tolerant)

Planting Method

Use healthy mature stems (25cm length, 6-8 nodes). Plant at 1m x 1m spacing on ridges or mounds. Plant at onset of major rains.

Weed Control

Weed at 3 and 8 weeks after planting. Mulching with dried grass recommended for moisture retention.

Harvesting

12-18 months after planting depending on variety. Test by digging around base. For gari production, harvest at 12 months.

üçÖ Vegetable Farming

Tomato Production

Nursery: Raise seedlings for 4 weeks in sterilized soil. Transplant at 60cm x 40cm spacing. Varieties: Power, Rasta, Petomech

Pepper Production

Varieties: Legon 18, Kpakpo shito, Bird's eye. Nursery period: 5-6 weeks. Space at 50cm x 40cm.

Irrigation

Drip irrigation recommended for dry season farming. Water requirement: 25-30mm per week. Mulching reduces water loss by 40%.

Integrated Pest Management

Use yellow sticky traps for whiteflies. Practice crop rotation with legumes. Neem extract effective against aphids.
